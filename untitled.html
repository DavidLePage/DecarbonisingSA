Why South Africa should be decarbonising its electricity supply as quickly as possible, and why existing fossil fuel subsidies to fossil fuel producers in South Africa should be phased out.
Summary of arguments
Risks from burning fossil fuels due to climate change
Global impacts of climate change: scope, severity, extent, feedbanks and runaway climate change. African impacts of climate change. Impacts on South Africa: drought, water, wildfires, migration, disease, etc.
Other risks of fossil fuels:
Air and water pollution. Evidence from Mpumalanga. Corruption in coal industry, cf state capture report. Destruction of agricultural land. Human rights issues. Premature deaths from air pollution.
Further benefits of decarbonisation
  • Building energy and water security
  • Increased jobs and rural development
  • Reducing financial risk to savers and investors
  • Reducing trade (possible tarriffs on embedded carbon) and technological risk
Extent of known fossil fuel subsidies in SA
  • State support in various forms
  • Support of Eskom for fossil fuel industry
  • Opportunity cost of fossil fuel subsidies, ie loss to public purse
  • ‘We can reduce extreme global poverty by three-fourths — right now [by ending fossil fuel subsidies]’. Vox article
Conclusion
Even if climate change didn’t exist, SA would still benefit greatly from decarbonising electricity. With climate change accepted, reasons ever greater. Removing fossil fuel subsidies vital to this process.
